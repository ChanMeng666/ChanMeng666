>

> 1. ≤280 字符（schema maxLength 限制）

@channel Hi everyone! :wave:
A student recently reached out with some fantastic questions about improving AI prompts and how to confidently approach job interviews when your portfolio projects are built with AI assistance.
I wanted to share the key takeaways with all of you, as mastering this mindset is essential for modern developers! :rocket:
1. Level Up Your Prompt Engineering :brain:
If you want to get better at communicating with AI or writing professional system prompts, the best approach is to study the official documentation. A highly recommended resource is the Claude Prompting Best Practices: https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/claude-prompting-best-practices
2. Rethink Architecture for the AI Era :building_construction:
One Monolith, One Codebase: Never build microservices or use multiple repos.
Why? A single monolithic codebase allows tools like Claude Code to understand your End-to-End (E2E) logic in one single context window. You control the global architecture; the AI handles the typing.
3. Acing Interviews with AI-Assisted Projects :briefcase:
It’s completely normal to feel anxious about technical interviews if you didn't hand-code every single line yourself. Here is the strategy you should use:
Embrace Transparency: Every modern tech company uses AI tools to accelerate development. If you try to hide or avoid discussing your experience with AI, it will actually count against you.
Focus on the "Why", Not the "How": Instead of stressing over deep, line-by-line code syntax, focus on the big picture. Be ready to confidently explain your project architecture and your specific decisions regarding the tech stack.
Highlight Your Product Vision: Interviewers want to see your analytical skills. Discuss your understanding of market needs, your business logic, and how you efficiently managed and communicated with AI to bring those features to life.
Real Projects > Toy Projects: Always emphasize that you are building real, production-grade applications, not just theoretical "toy" projects.
Shift Your Mindset: Learning to strictly hand-code today is like learning to drive a horse-drawn carriage in the age of automobiles. :racing_horse::car: Embrace your role as an architect and AI orchestrator!
Keep building, stay curious, and don't be afraid to lean into the AI era! :computer::sparkles:

@channel Hey everyone :wave:
If anyone based in Nigeria is experiencing issues accessing the Cursor website or getting blocked during sign-up, here’s a quick breakdown of why this is happening and how to get around it.
:magnifying_glass: Why is it blocked?
Automated Security Filters: Cursor isn’t intentionally blocking Nigeria. Their network security providers (like Cloudflare) often auto-block certain IP ranges to prevent bot abuse and DDoS attacks.
Strict Anti-Fraud Systems: Because AI compute costs are high, these tools have hyper-sensitive fraud prevention. Flagged IPs will automatically trigger ERR_CONNECTION_REFUSED or "Sign-up restricted" errors.
:hammer_and_spanner: How to fix it:
Use a VPN: Connecting through a US or EU server via a reliable VPN is the easiest workaround to access the site.
Switch nodes if registration fails: If you can open the site but get an error when creating an account, their system likely detected your proxy. Try switching to different (ideally less crowded) VPN nodes.
Tip: Once you successfully sign in to the local desktop IDE, daily coding and AI features usually work fine without needing constant verification.
Hope this helps anyone stuck on this! :rocket: @Precious
